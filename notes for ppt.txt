Abstract Slide:
Talk: "AstroNet introduces a novel approach that integrates astrocytes into neural networks, aiming to revolutionize how we optimize structure for improved accuracy and efficiency. Introduction Slide:
Talk: "Let's start with the challenges in neural network structure learning and the potential of astrocytes in neural regulation. This study is at the forefront of applying biological concepts to AI, which could unlock new levels of machine learning capabilities."

Main Objectives Slide:
Talk: "The primary objectives of AstroNet are clear: to propose a new model that incorporates astrocytic principles for network optimization, and to significantly enhance accuracy and efficiency in the learning process."

Key Contributions Slide:
Talk: "AstroNet's key contributions are truly groundbreaking. From the integration of astrocytes in the M-P neuron model to the development of the Astrocyte-Neuron model, this research promises to lead us to more efficient neural network optimization."

Methods Slide:
Talk: "Now, let's look at the methods. AstroNet's methodology is rooted in the Astrocyte-Neuron model, including temporal regulation and global connection mechanisms. These contribute to network optimization in a way that mimics biological processes."

Architecture of AstroNet Slide:
Talk: "The architecture of AstroNet is where the magic happens. It comprises a neural network (NN) and an astrocytes network (AN), with each layer designed to optimize the feature matrix, inspired by the functionality of astrocytes in the brain."

Experimental Results Slide:
Talk: "The experimental results are compelling. AstroNet was rigorously tested on ImageNet, CIFAR, and MNIST, showcasing superior performance in both accuracy and efficiency when benchmarked against state-of-the-art methods."

Conclusion Slide:
Talk: "In conclusion, AstroNet represents a paradigm shift in neural network design. Our results on standard datasets like ImageNet, CIFAR, and MNIST indicate that this novel approach has the potential to surpass current state-of-the-art methods."
